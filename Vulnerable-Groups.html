Vulnerable Groups

Support and resources for vulnerable individuals and communities

Vulnerable groups include individuals and communities who may face additional challenges due to factors such as age, disability, socioeconomic status, or other circumstances. This page provides resources and support to help these groups access the care and assistance they need.

Who Are Vulnerable Groups?

Vulnerable groups can include:

  • Children and young people.
  • Elderly individuals.
  • People with disabilities.
  • Individuals experiencing homelessness.
  • Refugees and asylum seekers.
  • Those living in poverty or facing financial hardship.

Challenges Faced by Vulnerable Groups

Vulnerable groups often face unique challenges, such as:

  • Limited access to healthcare and social services.
  • Social isolation and exclusion.
  • Higher risk of abuse or exploitation.
  • Difficulty accessing education or employment opportunities.
  • Stigma and discrimination.

Emergency Contacts

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger or needs urgent assistance, please call 999 (UK emergency number). For non-emergency support, you can contact the following:

  • Childline: 0800 1111 (for children and young people)
  • Samaritans: 116 123 (for emotional support)
  • Shelter: 0808 800 4444 (for housing and homelessness support)
