Support for Parents

Practical advice and resources for parents and carers

This page offers parents and carers valuable resources with practical advice on supporting children with mental health challenges or learning differences, such as autism or ADHD. It provides guidance on effective coping strategies and ways to nurture and empower your child’s development.

Below you will find some advice:

Look after yourself; if you are not taking care of yourself, you will not be able to care for your child.

Your mental health needs just as much care as your physical health.

You are not alone; you may feel isolated at times, but there are many parents in the same boat as you.

Try to meet other people in support groups, or even try therapy.

Don't forget that you know your child best!

Spend time understanding your child’s condition and how this may affect their behaviours/daily life.

Honour your feelings; don't repress your emotions for the sake of others. It's okay to cry sometimes.

Let people help you; having someone you and your child trust can be a huge aid.
